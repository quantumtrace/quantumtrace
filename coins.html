How it works - A Quantum Random Number Generator

The first step is to initialiaze a 2 qubit quantum register (since we have 2 coins) and a 2 bit classical register (since we want to store 2 results, after the measurements). If we measured every qubit at this point, we'd find 100% the qubits to be in the composite state |00⟩.

Then we apply a Hadamard gate to all qubits. This gate put a qubit in to a superposition of 0 and 1 with equal probability. So the 2-qubit system has a 25% of probability to be in one of the possible states. They are 4, i.e. 2 (possible outcomes per coin) at the power of 2 (number of coins). They'd be 8, if we had three coins.

Then we measure every qubit and this measurement will collapse the qubit to either 1 or 0, which we store in the 2 classical registers.
